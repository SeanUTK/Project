Climate Change and Resilience in Central Asia

Annual Newsletter

To solve the existing problems related to climate change, UNDP, with the financial support of the EU, is implementing a regional project "Climate Change and Resilience in Central Asia". The aim of the project is to support the achievement of stability and resilience to climate change in the Fergana Valley (border area of the Kyrgyz Republic, Tajikistan and Uzbekistan). In addition, the project initiates and promotes a regional climate change dialogue involving national government agencies.

Latest News

Within 2022, we established dialogue on climate change and promoted through workshops, a series of webinars, and other meetings at national and regional levels. Overall, organized 27 different events engaging 1258 people (375 women and 883 men).

A regional training on Climate Risk Management and Climate Adaptation was conducted in the Ferghana region of Uzbekistan. Three-day training identified possible ways of cooperation among government agencies and partner organizations of the of the Kyrgyz Republic, Tajikistan, and Uzbekistan.

Climate Change and Resilience in Central Asia provided a technical assistance to the Ministry of Water Resources of Uzbekistan on integrating climate and gender considerations into the draft Water Code of Uzbekistan. Moreover, project team is contributing to the development of sectoral NAP being a part of Uzhydromet's working group meetings.

Over 130 delegates from Central Asia and other stakeholders were engaged in a High-Level Dialogue “Climate Change and Resilience” around environmental threats and called on all Central Asian states to join forces to develop a sound balance between mitigation and adaptation to climate change

Read More >

About 209 young professionals from 64 universities in Tajikistan, Uzbekistan, and the Kyrgyz Republic took part in a regional scientific article contest on water-energy-food-health and climate change NEXUS. Out of 209 (of them 35% women) applications the commission selected 11 winners of them 6 girls or 66% who raised its capacity and knowledge in climate-related resilience risks and sectoral interlinkage

Project team in Tajikistan jointly with Helvetas independent Swiss development organization, Committee of Ecology and Environmental protection, Administration of Sughd region and the Arhus Center organized awareness campaign on climate change and water resources. As a part of the campaign, trees were planted, and a special bin were installed for collecting plastic waste in the bank of Syrdarya River.

The Project developed infographic on the level of awareness on climate-fragility risks in the Ferghana Valley and disseminated among 100 beneficiaries and stakeholders.

Read More >

A baseline awareness assessment of climate-related implications covered 415 respondents (65 women and 350 men) from Batkent, Osh, Jalal-Abad (Kyrgyz Republic), Andijan, Namangan, Ferghana (Uzbekistan) and Sughd (Tajikistan) regions. It revealed the level of awareness of decision makers about climate risks and resilience 21%3 in Uzbekistan, 23% in Kyrgyzstan and 21% in Tajikistan.

The project completed 3 national policy gap analysis in the Kyrgyz Republic, Tajikistan, and Uzbekistan with a review of policy documents, strategies and plans on climate change and resilience.
